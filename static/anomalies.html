Anomaly Detection

Identify months that deviate significantly from the historical monthly average (z-score threshold)

▶ How does anomaly detection work?
① What is a σ (sigma)?
σ (sigma) is a standard deviation — a unit that measures how far a value is from the average. A value of 0σ is exactly average. +2σ means 2 standard deviations above normal — unusually high. −2σ means unusually low.
② How is it calculated?
For each calendar month (e.g. all Julys ever recorded), the system calculates the long-term average and spread. Each month's observation is then scored:
σ = (value − monthly mean) ÷ std deviation
③ What does the threshold do?
Only months exceeding the threshold are flagged. At ±2σ roughly 5% of months are anomalous by chance. Raise it to ±3σ to see only the most extreme events (less than 0.3% probability). Lower it to ±1σ to see subtle deviations.
④ What can you find?
Try Heathrow · Max Temperature · ±2σ — the 2003 European heatwave (Aug 2003) should appear near the top. Or Durham · Rainfall · ±2σ to find the wettest and driest months on record.
