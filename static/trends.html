Climate Trends

Linear regression on any climate variable over time with decadal slope and R² goodness-of-fit

▶ How do I read this page?
① What is a trend line?
The dashed line is a linear regression — a best-fit straight line through all the data points. It shows the overall direction of change, ignoring year-to-year fluctuations. A rising red line = warming/increasing. A falling green line = cooling/decreasing.
② Trend per decade
This tells you how much the variable has changed per 10-year period on average. For example, +0.26°C/decade means temperatures have risen about 0.26°C every 10 years — so roughly +2°C over 75 years.
③ What is R²?
R² (R-squared) measures how well the trend line fits the data — from 0.0 to 1.0. 1.0 = perfect straight-line fit. 0.0 = no relationship at all. Climate data is noisy, so an R² of 0.1–0.3 can still indicate a real long-term trend.
④ Try this
Select Durham · Mean Temperature · 1900–2024 for a striking long-term warming signal. Try Oxford · Rainfall to see whether UK rainfall has changed over a century. Shorter date ranges will show noisier, less certain trends.
